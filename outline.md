# MUS 601: outline

Tuesdays, 7:00–9:00 pm, Fall 2026 (Aug 18 to Dec 1). Fifteen meetings across sixteen Tuesdays, with no class on the Nov 24 recess. Full readings, directed questions, outcomes, assessment, and policies live in `syllabus.md`.

**week 1. Aug 18. introduction.** Graduate theory and analysis review, and a first pass at the research and writing apparatus.

## unit 1: modal practice and the emergence of tonality (weeks 2–6)

- weeks 2 & 3. Aug 25, Sep 1. Renaissance modal and hexachordal theory (Josquin, la Rue, Rore, Lasso).
- weeks 4 & 5. Sep 8, Sep 15. early Baroque, modality to key-centered tonality (Monteverdi, Schütz).
- **week 6. Sep 22. paper 1 due.** Comparison: Lasso, Monteverdi, Schütz.

## unit 2: the late Baroque concerto and ritornello form (weeks 7–9)

- weeks 7 & 8. Sep 29, Oct 6. the instrumental concerto of the late Baroque.
- **week 9. Oct 13. paper 2 due.** Comparison: Vivaldi RV 396, Bach BWV 1055.

## unit 3: Classicism, sonata procedure, and Beethoven (weeks 10–14)

- weeks 10 & 11. Oct 20, Oct 27. mature Classicism and sonata procedure (Haydn 84, Mozart 36 "Linz").
- **week 12. Nov 3. paper 3 due.** Comparison: Mozart 41 "Jupiter," Haydn 90, first movements.
- week 13. Nov 10. Beethoven and the extension of Classical procedure.
- **week 14. Nov 17. paper 4 due.** Beethoven, "Kreutzer" Sonata Op. 47, first movement.

*Nov 24: fall recess, no class.*

## unit 4: Schubert and early Romantic chromaticism (week 15 and finals)

- week 15. Dec 1. Schubert and early Romanticism.
- **finals week. Dec 7–12. paper 5 due.** Schubert, "Death and the Maiden" Quartet, first movement.
